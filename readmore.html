Reduction in Deforestation:

Recycling one ton of paper saves approximately 17 trees from being cut down. (Source: Environmental Paper Network)

In India, the paper industry is one of the largest consumers of wood, leading to deforestation and habitat loss. Recycling paper helps mitigate this impact by reducing the demand for virgin wood pulp. (Source: Centre for Science and Environment)

PollutionReduction :

Paper production is a significant source of air and water pollution, with emissions of greenhouse gases, particulate matter, and wastewater containing harmful chemicals. By recycling paper, these pollution emissions can be reduced significantly. (Source: United Nations Environment Programme)

In India, the recycling of paper and other materials helps divert waste from landfills, reducing the release of methane, a potent greenhouse gas, and decreasing soil and water pollution. (Source: Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change, Government of India)

Global Perspective:

According to the World Bank, global paper consumption has tripled over the past three decades, leading to increased pressure on forests and ecosystems.

Recycling paper is a crucial strategy for mitigating these impacts and conserving natural resources. The Ellen MacArthur Foundation estimates that if the global paper industry were to achieve 70% recycling rates by 2050, it could lead to a 55% reduction in greenhouse gas emissions compared to business-as-usual scenarios.
